DoS: Denial of Service

Denial of Service (DoS) attacks are cyber attacks that aim to make a computer or network resource unavailable to its intended users by temporarily or indefinitely disrupting services. These attacks typically target web servers, networks, and services by overwhelming them with a flood of illegitimate requests or traffic.

DoS attacks can be launched using various techniques, including flooding the target with excessive traffic, exploiting vulnerabilities in software, or exhausting system resources. They can have serious consequences, causing downtime, financial losses, and damage to reputation.

Types of DoS Attacks

Some common types of DoS attacks include:

  • UDP Flood
  • Syn Flood
  • HTTP Flood
  • Slowloris Attack

Prevention and Mitigation

Organizations can mitigate the impact of DoS attacks by implementing strategies such as:

  • Network monitoring and traffic filtering
  • Using firewalls and intrusion detection systems
  • Implementing rate limiting and access controls
  • Utilizing content delivery networks (CDNs)
